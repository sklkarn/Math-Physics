Properties:.

Vector triple product is not associative, i.e., \(\vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})} \neq (\vec{a}\times{\vec{b})\times\vec{c}}\text{.}\)
\begin{align} \because \quad \vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})}\amp = \left( \vec{a}\cdot\vec{c}\right)\vec{b} - \left( \vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}\right)\vec{c}\tag{1.1.13} \end{align}
\begin{align*} \text{and}\quad \vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})} \amp = -\vec{c}\times{(\vec{a}\times\vec{b})} = -\left[\left( \vec{c}\cdot\vec{b}\right)\vec{a} - \left( \vec{c}\cdot\vec{a}\right)\vec{b}\right] \end{align*}
\begin{equation} \therefore \quad \vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})} = \left(\vec{a}\cdot\vec{c}\right)\vec{b} -\left(\vec{b}\cdot\vec{c}\right)\vec{a}\tag{1.1.14} \end{equation}
hence, \(\vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})} \neq (\vec{a}\times{\vec{b})\times\vec{c}}\text{.}\)
Equation (1.1.13) shows that a vector \(\vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})}\) is lying in the plane of \(\vec{b}\) and \(\vec{c}\text{.}\) While equation (1.1.14) shows a vector \(\vec{a}\times{(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})}\) is lying in the plane of \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\text{.}\)
