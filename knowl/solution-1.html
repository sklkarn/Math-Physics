Solution 1.1.5.1.

Let \(\vec{a} = \hat{i}-3\hat{j}+2\hat{k}\text{,}\) \(\vec{b} = 2\hat{i}-4\hat{j}-\hat{k}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{c}=3\hat{i}+2\hat{j}-\hat{k}\text{.}\)
If these vectors are linearly dependent then \(l\vec{a} + m\vec{b}+ n\vec{c} =0\text{.}\) where \(l, m, n\) are scalars, and at least one scalar being non-zero, i.e.,
\begin{align*} l( \hat{i}-3\hat{j}+2\hat{k}) + m(2\hat{i}-4\hat{j}-\hat{k}) + n(3\hat{i}+2\hat{j}-\hat{k}) \amp = 0\\ \text{or,} \quad (l+2m+3n)\hat{i} + (-3l-4m+2n)\hat{j} + (2l-m-n)\hat{k}\amp =0 \end{align*}
Equating the coefficients of \(\hat{i}\text{,}\) \(\hat{j}\text{,}\) and \(\hat{k}\) on both sides, we get-
\begin{equation} l+2m+3n =0\tag{1.1.7} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} -3l-4m+2n =0\tag{1.1.8} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{and} \quad 2l-m-n =0 \tag{1.1.9} \end{equation}
From eqns. (1.1.7) and (1.1.8) we get-
\begin{align*} \frac{l}{4+12}\amp =\frac{m}{-9-2} =\frac{n}{-4+6} =\lambda \quad \text{(say)} \\ \text{or,} \quad \frac{l}{16} \amp =\frac{m}{-11}=\frac{n}{2} =\lambda\\ \text{or,}\quad l \amp =16\lambda, \quad m = -11 \lambda,\quad \text{and}\quad n =2\lambda \end{align*}
substituting these values in eqn.(1.1.9) we get -
\begin{align*} 41\lambda \amp =0,\quad \Rightarrow \lambda = 0, \\ \therefore \quad l\amp =m =n =0. \end{align*}
Hence the vectors are linearly independent.
