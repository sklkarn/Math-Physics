Item IV.

If z is a function of two independent variables x and y i.e., \(z=f(x,y)\) then \(\frac{\,dz}{\,dx}\) and \(\frac{\,dz}{\,dy} \) is partial derivatives of z with respect to x and y respectively. Hence the differential equation containing these partial derivatives are the partial differential equation, e.g.,
\begin{equation*} \frac{\,dz}{\,dx}+\frac{\,dz}{\,dy} =x^{2}. \end{equation*}
