Let \(\phi\) be the scalar function of orthogonal curvilinear coordinates \(u,v,w\text{,}\) then,
\begin{equation} \,grad \phi=\vec{\nabla}\phi = f_{1}\hat{e}_{1}+f_{2}\hat{e}_{2}+f_{3}\hat{e}_{3}\tag{1.6.7} \end{equation}
where \(f_{1},f_{2},f_{3}\) are to be determined. We know that
\begin{equation*} \vec{\,dr}= h_{1}\hat{e}_{1}\,du+h_{2}\hat{e}_{2}\,dv+h_{3}\hat{e}_{3}\,dw \end{equation*}
Now,
\begin{equation} \,d\phi=\vec{\nabla}\phi\cdot\vec{\,dr}=h_{1}f_{1}\,du+h_{2}f_{2}\,dv+h_{3}f_{3}\,dw\tag{1.6.8} \end{equation}
As, \(\phi=\phi(u,v,w)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \,d\phi= \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial u}\,du+\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial v}\,dv+\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial w}\,dw\tag{1.6.9} \end{equation}
from eqns. (1.6.8) and (1.6.9), we get -
\begin{equation*} h_{1}f_{1}=\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial u}\Rightarrow f_{1} = \frac{1}{h_{1}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial u} \end{equation*}
Similarly,
\begin{equation*} f_{2} = \frac{1}{h_{2}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial v}, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} f_{3} = \frac{1}{h_{3}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial w} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad \vec{\nabla}\phi =\frac{1}{h_{2}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial u}\hat{e_{1}}+ \frac{1}{h_{2}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial v}\hat{e_{2}} + \frac{1}{h_{3}}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial w}\hat{e_{3}} \end{equation*}
This indicates the del operator as
\begin{equation*} \left[\vec{\nabla} = \frac{\hat{e_{1}}}{h_{1}}\frac{\partial}{\partial u}+ \frac{\hat{e_{2}}}{h_{2}}\frac{\partial}{\partial v}+\frac{\hat{e_{3}}}{h_{3}}\frac{\partial}{\partial w}\right] \end{equation*}
