Now,
\begin{equation*} AX=\lambda X =(\lambda I)X \end{equation*}
or,
\begin{equation} (A-\lambda I)X = 0\tag{2.5.1} \end{equation}
where \(I\) is a unit matrix, \(X\) is an eigen vector or eigen function of an operator \(A\) and the constant \(\lambda\) is an eigen value. when \(A\) is represented by a square matrix, then the eigen values of a matrix \(A\) are determined by an eqn. (2.5.1) is called a secular or a characteristic equation.
