A square plate has its faces and its edges \(x=0\) and \(x=\pi\) for \((0 \lt y \lt \pi)\) is insulated. Its edges \(y=0\) and \(y=\pi\) are kept at temperatures zero and \(f(x)\) respectively. Show that the steady temperature is given by
\begin{equation*} u(x,y) = \frac{1}{2\pi}a_{o}y+\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}a_{n}\frac{\sinh ny}{\sinh n\pi} \cos nx \end{equation*}
where
\begin{equation*} a_{n}=\frac{2}{\pi}\int\limits_{0}^{\pi}f(x) \sin\cos nx\,dx, \quad n=0,1,2,3,\cdots. \end{equation*}
