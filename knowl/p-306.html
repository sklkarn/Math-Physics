When an operator \(A\) acts on a vector \(X\) then the resulting vector \(AX\) is different from \(X\text{.}\) However there may exist certain non-zero vectors for which \(AX\) is just multiplied by a constant \(\lambda\text{.}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} AX=\lambda X \end{equation*}
is known as an eigen value problem.
