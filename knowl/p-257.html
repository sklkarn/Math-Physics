  1. Transpose conjugate of a matrix is the same as conjugate of its transpose, i.e., \(A^{\dagger} = (A^{*})^{t} = (A^{t})^{*}\text{.}\)
  2. Transpose conjugate of the conjugate transpose of a matrix is the matrix itself, i.e., \((A^{\dagger})^{\dagger}= A\text{.}\)
  3. Hermitian conjugate of the sum of two matrices is the sum of their Hermitian conjugate, i.e., \((A+B)^{\dagger} = A^{\dagger} +B^{\dagger}\text{.}\)
  4. Hermitian conjugate of the product of two matrices is the product of their Hermitian conjugates in reverse order, i.e., \((AB)^{\dagger} = B^{\dagger} A^{\dagger}\text{.}\)
  5. Hermitian conjugate of scalar multiple of a matrix is the scalar multiple of its comples conjugate, i.e., \((\alpha A)^{\dagger} = \alpha^{*} A^{\dagger}\text{,}\) where \(\alpha\) is a complex number.
