The eqns. (3.0.1) and (3.0.2) define a transformation of coordinates from one frame to another. Differentiating eqn. (3.0.1), we get -
\begin{equation*} \,d\bar{x}^{i} = \frac{\partial \bar{x}^{i} }{\partial x^{1}}\,dx^{1} + \frac{\partial \bar{x}^{i} }{\partial x^{2}}\,dx^{2} +\cdots +\frac{\partial \bar{x}^{i} }{\partial x^{n}}\,dx^{n} = \sum\limits_{j=1}^{n}\frac{\partial \bar{x}^{i} }{\partial x^{j}}\,dx^{j} \end{equation*}
or, simply,
\begin{equation} \,d\bar{x}^{i} = \frac{\partial \bar{x}^{i} }{\partial x^{j}}\,dx^{j}\tag{3.0.3} \end{equation}
Eqn. (3.0.3) is a coordinate transformation equation.
