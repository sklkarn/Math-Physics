  1. The transpose of the transpose of a matrix \(A\) is the matrix itself \(A\text{,}\) i.e., \((A^{t})^{t} = A\text{.}\)
  2. The transpose of the sum of two matrices is the sum of their transposes, i.e., \((A+B)^{t}=A^{t}+B^{t}\text{.}\)
  3. The transpose of a scalar times the matrix is the scalar times the transpose of the matrix, i.e., \((kA)^{t} = kA^{t}\) where \(k\) is a scalar.
  4. The transpose of the product of two matrices is the product in reverse order of their transpose, i.e., \((AB)^{t} = B^{t}A^{t}\text{.}\)
  5. The magnitude of transpose of a matrix is the magnitude of the matrix, i.e., \(|A^{t}| = |A|\text{.}\)
