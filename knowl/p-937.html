Convolution theorem is a mathematical operation widely used in communication physics. If \(F(s)\) and \(G(s)\) are Fourier transform of two functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) respectively, then Fourier transform of the convolution (coiled) of \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) is the product of their Fourier transforms, i.e.
\begin{equation*} \mathscr{F} \{f(x) * g(x)\} = F(s).G(s) \end{equation*}
where the astric \(($*$)\) denotes convolution (not a complex conjugate).
