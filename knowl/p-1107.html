If the acceleration of the element along vertical be \(\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial t^{2}}\) and mass of an element AB is \(m\,ds\) then according to Newton's second law of motion, this force is given by
\begin{equation} F_{y} = m\,ds\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial t^{2}}\tag{7.1.4} \end{equation}
from equations (7.1.3) and (7.1.4), we have-
\begin{equation*} T\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial x^{2}}\,dx = m\,dx\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial t^{2}} \end{equation*}
[Since, slope of the curve is small, \(\,ds\) can be replaced by \(\,dx\text{.}\)]
\begin{equation*} T\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial x^{2}} = m\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial t^{2}} \end{equation*}
or,
\begin{equation} \frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial x^{2}} = \frac{1}{v^{2}}\frac{\partial^{2} y}{\partial t^{2}}\tag{7.1.5} \end{equation}
It is the wave equation for a vibrating string, where \(v^{2}=\frac{T}{m}\) is the velocity of the wave propagating along the string.
