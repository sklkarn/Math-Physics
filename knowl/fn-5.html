Footnote 4.4.1.

Let
\begin{equation*} \theta =a_{o}x^{m} \end{equation*}
then.
\begin{equation*} \log\theta = \log a_{o}+m\log x \end{equation*}
or,
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\theta}\partial\theta =0+\log x\partial m \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \frac{\partial\theta}{\partial m } = \theta\log x. \end{equation*}
