Commutative law:
\begin{equation*} \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \vec{b} + \vec{a} \end{equation*}
Let \(\vec{OA} = \vec{a}\) and \(\vec{OB} = \vec{b}\) be the adjacent sides of a parallelogram of diagonal vector \(\vec{OC}\) as shown in FigureÂ 1.1.1.(b), then
\begin{equation} \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \vec{OA} + \vec{AC} = \vec{OC}\tag{1.1.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \text{also,} \quad \vec{b} + \vec{a} = \vec{OB} + \vec{BC} = \vec{OC} \tag{1.1.2} \end{equation}
from (1.1.1) and (1.1.2), we have -
\begin{equation*} \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \vec{b} + \vec{a} \end{equation*}
