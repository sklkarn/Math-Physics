Any vector \(\vec{r}\) in a space can be resolved into three rectangular components. Let the position vector of a point \(P (x,y,z)\) be \(\vec{OP}= \vec{r}\text{,}\) the diagonal vector of a parallelopiped and \(\hat{i}\text{,}\) \(\hat{j}\text{,}\) and \(\hat{k}\) are the unit vectors along \(OX, OY\) and \(OZ\) axes, respectively so that \(\vec{OA} = x \hat{i}\text{,}\) \(\vec{OB} = y \hat{j}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{OC}= z \hat{k}\text{,}\) the rectangular components of \(\vec{r}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \text{Now,}\quad \vec{r} \amp = \vec{OP} = (\vec{OF} + \vec{FP})\\ \amp = (\vec{OA} + \vec{AF}) + \vec{FP}= \vec{OA} + \vec{OB} + \vec{OC} \\ \amp = x \hat{i} + y \hat{j} + z \hat{k}. \end{align*}
