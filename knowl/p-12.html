Let a vector \(\vec{a}\) is multiplied by any real positive number \(n\text{,}\) then the product \(n \vec{a}\) is a vector whose magnitude is \(n\) times as that of \(\vec{a}\) and its direction is the same as \(\vec{a}\text{.}\) If \(n\) is a negative number then the direction of \(n \vec{a}\) is opposite to \(\vec{a}\text{.}\) If \(\hat{a}\) is a unit vector in the direction of \(\vec{a}\) then, we have
  1. \(\displaystyle \vec{a} = |a| \hat{a}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle (k+l)\vec{a} = (k+l)a \hat{a} = k a \hat{a} + l a \hat{a} = k \vec{a} + l \vec{a}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle k(\vec{a} + \vec{b}) = k \vec{a} + k\vec{b}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle k(l\vec{a}) = (k l) \vec{a}\)
