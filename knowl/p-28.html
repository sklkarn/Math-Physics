From eqns. (1.1.10) and (1.1.12) we get -
\begin{align*} \frac{l}{-1+16}=\frac{m}{-12+2} =\frac{n}{-8+3} \amp = \lambda \quad \text{(say)}\\ \text{or,} \quad \frac{l}{15} =\frac{m}{-10} =\frac{n}{-5} \amp=\lambda \\ \text{or,} \quad l=15\lambda, \quad m = -10 \lambda, \quad \text{and} \quad n \amp =-5\lambda \end{align*}
These values of \(l, m, n \) satisfies the eqn.(1.1.11), hence for any set of scalars (all of them being not zero) of the given vectors \(\vec{a}\text{,}\) \(\vec{b}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{c}\) are linearly dependent.
