The divergence theorem is
\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_{S} \vec{A}\cdot\vec{dS}=\iiint\limits_{V} div\vec{A} \,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \iiint\limits_{V} \left(\hat{i}\frac{\partial}{\partial x}+\hat{j}\frac{\partial}{\partial y}+\hat{k}\frac{\partial}{\partial z}\right)\cdot (x^{3}\hat{i}+ y^{3}\hat{j}+z^{3}\hat{k})\,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\iiint\limits_{V} \left(3x^{2}+ 3y^{2}+3z^{2}\right)dV= 3\iiint\limits_{V} \left(x^{2}+ y^{2}+z^{2}\right)\,dV \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =3\iiint\limits_{V}a^{2}dV= 3a^{2}\iiint\limits_{V}\,dV = 3a^{2}\left(\frac{4}{3}\pi a^{3}\right) = 4\pi a^{5}. \end{equation*}
