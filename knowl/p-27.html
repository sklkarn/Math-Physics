Let \(\vec{a} =2\hat{i}+\hat{j}-3\hat{k}\text{,}\) \(\vec{b}= \hat{i}-4\hat{k}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{c} =4\hat{i}+3\hat{j}-\hat{k}\) If these vectors are linearly dependent, then \(l\vec{a} + m\vec{b}+ n\vec{c} =0\) where \(l, m, \text{and} n\) are scalars and at least one scalar being not zero, i.e.,
\begin{align*} l(2\hat{i}+\hat{j}-3\hat{k}) + m(\hat{i}-4\hat{k}) + n(4\hat{i}+3\hat{j}-\hat{k}) \amp=0\\ \text{or,} \quad (2l+m+4n)\hat{i}+(l+3n)\hat{j}+(-3l-4m-n)\hat{k} \amp =0 \end{align*}
Equating the coefficients of \(\hat{i}\text{,}\) \(\hat{j}\text{,}\) and \(\hat{k}\) on both sides, we get-
\begin{align} 2l+m+4n \amp =0\tag{1.1.10}\\ l+0m+3n \amp =0 \tag{1.1.11}\\ \text{and,} \quad -3l-4m-n \amp =0 \tag{1.1.12} \end{align}
