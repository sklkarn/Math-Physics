Distributive Law:
\begin{equation*} \vec{a}\cdot{(\vec{b}+\vec{c})}= \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{b}} + \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{c}} \end{equation*}
Let \(\vec{OA} =\vec{a}\text{,}\) \(\vec{OB} =\vec{b}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{BC} =\vec{c}\) as shown in FigureÂ 1.1.7.(b), then
\begin{equation*} \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{b}} + \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{c}} = \vec{OA}\cdot {\vec{OB}} + \vec{OA}\cdot {\vec{BC}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = OA\cdot{OM}+OA\cdot{MN} = OA(OM+MN) = OA.ON \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \vec{a}\cdot{(\vec{b}+\vec{c})} = \vec{OA}\cdot{(\vec{OB}+\vec{BC})} = \vec{OA}\cdot{\vec{OC}} = OA.ON \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad \vec{a}\cdot{(\vec{b}+\vec{c})} = \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{b}} + \vec{a}\cdot {\vec{c}} \end{equation*}
